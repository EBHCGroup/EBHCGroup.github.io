Get Educated

Developing a new standard in distance learning.

The EBHC Group is developing distance learning educational programs that will help educational institutions teach evidence-based healthcare. This program will implement state of the art distance learning technologies to facilitate both asynchronous and synchronous learning platforms.

  • Introduction to EBHC

  • Strategic Literature Searching
  • Study Design
  • Critical Appraisal
  • Medical Statistics
  • Implementation

Hybrid courses

This one semester program combines the online course with faculty-facilitated small group sessions. Faculty members from your educational institution will receive training and coaching on leading small group activities. This will enable students to build competency in specific skill-sets critical to implementing evidence-based healthcare.

Get Trained

Spreading high quality medicine world-wide

Evidence-based healthcare is the standard for the practice of contemporary, high-quality medicine throughout the world. U.S. healthcare schools, including medicine, dentistry and allied healthcare professionals, all offer a curriculum in EBHC.

Recent changes in the U.S. healthcare education standards now require healthcare education programs to offer an EBHC curriculum, a change that has accelerated interest in program and curriculum development in this area. Having an effective and comprehensive teaching program in EBHC is becoming the standard in all healthcare education programs worldwide, much as it has become in medicine over the last decade.

The EBHC Group offers customized training programs to help members of healthcare organizations, including educational institutions, develop a greater understanding of EBHC. These programs provide the skill development needed to successfully and efficiently implement EBHC. They also provide a basis of skills needed to teach EBHC to faculty, residents and students. Training programs are typically 2-5 days or more, and are customized to the individual school's specific needs.

 

Learn to Implement

Implementation is the key to success

Implementation is the key to developing an EBHC approach to treating patients. However, research indicates that increasing knowledge is not sufficient to motivate change to EBHC. There are many barriers, including both individual and institutional barriers that may prevent progress. EBHC Group offers customized implementation programs to help organizations succeed. Our programs include:

  • Needs Assessment
  • Strategic Implementation Plan Development
  • Customized Education and Training Programs
  • Program Evaluation

Elliot Abt, DDS, MS, MSc

Elliot Abt received a bachelor's degree in molecular biology from the University of Wisconsin-Madison. He went on to graduate training and received a master's degree in oral pathology and dental degree from the University of Illinois.

Dr. Abt completed his dental residency training at Ravenswood and the University of Illinois Hospitals. From 1989-2004, Dr. Abt was the director of the dental residency programs at Ravenswood and Illinois Masonic Medical Center. He later went back to graduate school and received a master's degree in evidence-based healthcare from Oxford University.

Dr. Abt is on the editorial board of the Journal of Evidence-Based Dental Practice, and has published articles and book chapters on research design, methodology, and statistical analysis of the dental literature. He has also participated in several ADA systemic reviews and clinical practice guidelines and is a current member of the ADA EBD Faculty Development Program.

Dr. Abt is an adjunct associate professor of oral medicine at the University of Illinois and directs the journal club for dental residents at Illinois Masonic Medical Center. He is on the Critical Review Panel of the American Dental Association (ADA) Center for Evidence-Based Dentistry and teaches research methodology and biostatistics at the ADA/Forsyth Advanced EBD Course. Dr. Abt is in private dental practice and is a member of the ADA Council on Scientific Affairs.

Julie Frantsve-Hawley, PhD

Julie Hawley, PhD, is the Executive Director of the American Association of Public Health Dentistry (AAPHD) and an Adjunct Clinical Assistant Professor at the University of Illinois College of Dentistry. Prior to joining AAPHD, Dr. Hawley directed the American Dental Association’s Center for Evidence-based Dentistry for 10 years where she developed and launched many of the ADA’s EBD initiatives, including the ADA’s EBD website, the ADA evidence-based practice guidelines program, and multiple educational programs.

Dr. Hawley has lead organizing committees and been a head instructor for educational programs for practicing dentists, dental school faculty, and students. Examples include the ADA’s flagship EBD Champions Conference, the Advanced EBD Workshop, and online tutorials. She also led efforts to develop and teach multiple custom EBD training programs for dental school faculty throughout the US.

Dr. Hawley has been the PI on several grants from the National Institute for Dental and Craniofacial Research, the National Library of Medicine, and the Agency for Healthcare Research and Quality. She is involved in dissemination and implementation research aimed at helping implement science into practice at the individual and program levels.

Dr. Hawley received a bachelor’s degree in biology from Loyola University of Chicago and a PhD from Harvard University’s Biological and Biomedical Sciences program. She conducted a postdoctoral fellowship at the University of California, San Francisco, and attended the Medical Informatics program at Woods Hole and the Summer Institute at the Alan Alda Center for Communicating Science at Stony Brook University. She is also the editor of a textbook on Evidence-Based Dentistry published by Quintessence.

Robert J Weyant, MS, DMD, DrPH

Robert J. Weyant is professor and chair of the Department of Dental Public Health and associate dean for public health at the University of Pittsburgh School of Dental Medicine. He is the current editor-in-chief of the Journal of Public Health Dentistry. Dr. Weyant is the principle developer of the evidence-based dentistry program at the University of Pittsburgh that includes didactic, small group, and clinical EBD training of dental students. This training program has been in operation for over 15 years. This year Dr. Weyant was the inaugural recipient of the ADA/AADR Evidence-based Dentistry Faculty Teaching Award.

Dr. Weyant’s educational background related to EBD includes a doctoral degree in epidemiology from the University of Michigan and completion of EBD courses at the Boston Forsyth Center and Oxford University.

He has instructed for six years in the ADA Champions program and the ADA EBD Faculty Development Program. Dr. Weyant has been a mentor in the ADA's Critical Review Panel, is former associate editor of the Journal of Evidence-Based Dental Practice, and participated in the ADA development of several ADA systematic reviews and guidelines. Dr. Weyant tutored two courses on teaching evidence-based medicine at Oxford University.

Dr. Weyant maintains an NIH sponsored research program in dental epidemiology focusing on oral health disparities of caries in children and has authored or co-authored over 100 papers in the peer review literature as well as two textbook chapters on evidence-based dentistry.

In addition to his training in epidemiology, he holds, bachelor’s, master’s and dental degrees from the University of Pittsburgh and has been a diplomate of American Board of Dental Public Health since 1987.
